# Preferences and Constraints

## Schedule

- **Mornings (7-11 AM):** Protected deep work time. No meetings if possible. This is when the best thinking happens.
- **Afternoons:** Meetings, communications, collaborative work.
- **5 PM - 8 PM:** Present with Teddy (18-month-old daughter). Non-negotiable family time.
- **Weekends:** Family time. No work unless something is genuinely on fire.
- **Email:** Check in the morning, then stay out of it. Protect focus time.

## Work Style Preferences

- **Deep work over shallow work.** Morning hours are sacred for analysis and thinking.
- **Async over sync.** Teams message or email over a meeting, unless the meeting has a clear purpose.
- **Specificity over abstraction.** Show me the data, not the framework.
- **Iteration over perfection.** Prefer iterative diagnostic rounds over one big exploratory query. See each result before steering the next step.
- **Function over polish.** A working dashboard now beats a beautiful one next month.

## Strong Opinions

- **Max 2-3 data points per visual.** More than that causes confusion, not insight.
- **Never hire generalist agencies.** If someone says they're good at Facebook ads AND SEO AND Google Ads, they're average at all of them. Hire specialists for one thing.
- **Data first, strategy second.** Don't execute strategy based on broad assumptions. Test, measure, then decide.
- **AI is a creative partner, not a crutch.** Position AI as helping create something new, not as catching mistakes.
- **"Customer numbers" not "customers"** in public-facing data references. More precise.
- **Never name competitors** in LinkedIn content about analytical work.
- **Never mix stats from different analyses/populations** on the same deliverable.

## What Frustrates Me

- Wasting time on low-impact busywork.
- People executing strategy based on untested assumptions instead of data.
- Long explanations that bury the point.
- My own blind spot: I don't always handle negative outcomes well. I can shut down instead of channeling the energy productively. I'm aware of this and working on resilience.

## Non-Negotiable Principles

- **Internal locus of control.** I'm in control of my outcomes. If something isn't working, change the environment or change the approach. Don't sit around blaming circumstances.
- **Focus on what you can control.** Everything else is noise.
- **Be ruthlessly efficient or eliminate.** If a recurring task doesn't drive the business, automate it with AI or stop doing it.
- **Enjoy the work.** I don't believe in work-life balance as a concept. I believe in enjoying what you do so much that the boundary doesn't matter.
